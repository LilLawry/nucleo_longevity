# NUCLEO CONNECT — Monetization Roadmap

> How Connect earns without compromising independence. Every revenue line is
> designed so that **spend never buys a grade and never buys "verified"**.

## Principle
Editorial (grades) and commercial (Finder, Connect) are separated by a wall.
Connect monetizes **access, presentation, and advisory** — never trust itself.
Verification is earned through checks, not payment.

## Revenue lines (staged)

### Stage 1 — Curated advisory (live intent, low volume)
- Paid **introductions / advisory** between vetted manufacturers and
  distribution/sales partners (the existing brands/reps intake).
- Fees agreed upfront; no hidden margins; no pay-to-play on grades.
- Cash-flow first, minimal build.

### Stage 2 — Sponsored presence (directory)
- `commercialStatus = sponsored` / `premium`: enhanced placement or richer
  profile fields.
- **Guardrail:** sponsored chip always shown; sponsored never implies verified;
  sponsored profiles are still subject to the same review before publishing.

### Stage 3 — Verification services
- Paid **verification review** (source-checking declared claims/certs) — the fee
  buys the *work of checking*, not a guaranteed "verified" outcome. A failed
  check still costs the applicant and does not yield the badge.

### Stage 4 — Digital fair / expo
- Time-boxed themed showcases (`/connect/expo`), sponsorable slots, curated
  briefs in `/connect/opportunities`.

### Stage 5 — Data / licensing (optionality)
- Licensing the curated, structured ecosystem dataset or the methodology to a
  strategic partner — an exit-optionality lever, not a day-one product.

## What we will NOT monetize
- Grades (never for sale, never influenced).
- The "verified" label (earned by checks only).
- User personal/health data (not collected).
- Transactions between parties (Connect is not a marketplace).

## Guardrail checklist for any new Connect revenue idea
1. Does it keep grades independent? If no → reject.
2. Does it keep sponsored and verified visibly separate? If no → redesign.
3. Does it avoid accounts/payments/orders between third parties? If no → it's a
   marketplace, out of scope.
4. Is the buyer paying for *work or presentation*, not for *trust*? If not →
   reject.
5. Is the disclosure visible on the surface where money changes hands? If not →
   add it.

## Metrics
- Trust proxies: verified-partner count, source-checked ratio, complaint rate.
- Commercial: advisory deals closed, sponsored profiles, verification jobs.
- Independence audit: zero instances of grade change correlated with spend
  (tracked; must stay zero).
