evidenceSummary: "Hyaluronic acid is a natural component of skin and joint tissue that binds large amounts of water. The evidence splits by route: topical and injectable (dermal fillers, some osteoarthritis injections) use is well established for hydration and volume; oral supplementation has a growing set of small trials suggesting improved skin hydration and possibly joint comfort, though absorption and mechanism of ingested HA are debated. There is no systemic longevity evidence. Grade C: real, route-dependent cosmetic and joint benefits, not a longevity intervention."
mechanism: "A glycosaminoglycan that retains water to maintain tissue hydration and viscoelasticity; ingested HA is broken down and may act partly by signalling rather than direct incorporation."
