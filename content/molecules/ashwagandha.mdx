evidenceSummary: "Ashwagandha has a cluster of small randomized trials showing reductions in perceived stress and cortisol and improvements in sleep over 6–12 weeks, with some data on modest strength or testosterone changes in men. The effects are real but short-term and studied mostly with standardised extracts. It has no aging or longevity outcome data. Balanced against that are sporadic reports of drug-induced liver injury and thyroid effects. Grade C: a legitimate short-term stress/sleep aid, not a geroprotector and not risk-free."

safety: "Generally well tolerated short-term. Concerns include occasional GI upset, thyroid hormone changes, and rare but documented cases of liver injury. Avoid in pregnancy and with certain autoimmune/thyroid conditions without medical advice."
